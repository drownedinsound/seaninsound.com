A LIFE IN ACTIVISM, MEDIA & IMPACT

Tori Tsui

Climate justice activist · Author · Host · Advisor

From growing up in a fishing town in northeast Hong Kong where typhoons closed her school, to organising a benefit concert at Wembley Arena — a timeline of how Tori Tsui built one of the most remarkable activist careers of her generation.

Filter
1994

Born in Auckland, New Zealand

Origin

Grew up in a small fishing community in northeast Hong Kong. Eurasian heritage spanning Cantonese, Taiwanese, Macanese, Vietnamese, English, Welsh, Finnish and Turkish ancestry. Typhoons regularly prevented her from attending school — she didn't connect this to climate change until age 14 in the classroom.

2010s

University of Oxford

Education

Initially studied medicine; switched to Natural Sciences. First engagement with climate science in the classroom connected the dots on Hong Kong's pollution, typhoons and the wider crisis.

MRes, Ecology, Evolution & Conservation — Imperial College London

Education

2015–16. Soon after, she stepped away from the academic path and relocated to Bristol to work in natural history documentary production.

I just thought, you know what? Screw my career. There's a climate crisis.

Imperial College London

BBC presenter & production team

Career

2017–19, including BBC Springwatch. Natural history documentary work across two years before the tension between the industry's constraints and her values became untenable.

BBC Springwatch
2019

Extinction Rebellion — scouted at protest

Activism

Creating films on the biodiversity crisis at XR protests. Stella McCartney's creative director spotted her and invited her into the brand's world — a chance encounter that changed the trajectory of her public career.

Stella McCartney FW19 — 'Agents of Change' campaign

Media

Global campaign narrated by Jane Goodall, written by Jonathan Safran Foer, featuring Amber Valletta. Tori named an Agent of Change alongside major figures in sustainability — the campaign directly funded her voyage to COP25.

▶

Sailed Atlantic to COP25 — Sail to the COP

Landmark

Sponsored by Stella McCartney. Joined 36 young delegates aboard. COP25 was relocated from Santiago, Chile to Madrid due to civil unrest; the crew participated remotely from Martinique and docked in Cartagena, Colombia — undeterred, Tori spent the following four months working with Latin American and Caribbean youth on the ground.

2020

Stella McCartney Paris Fashion Week roundtable

Activism

1 March 2020, eve of the AW20 show at Palais Garnier, Paris. Spoke alongside Amber Valletta and Jonathan Safran Foer; Christiana Figueres (former UNFCCC Executive Secretary) as guest of honour. A gathering of fashion, climate policy and activism.

Unite for Climate Action (U4CA) — co-founded

Campaign

Spent four months in Colombia facilitating access for Latin American, Indigenous and Caribbean youth to UN climate intersessionals. The sailing project evolved into a transatlantic coalition. Collaborated with the German Ministry of the Environment.

Bad Activist Collective & podcast — co-founded

Media

Co-founded with Kisjonah Roos and Julia Gentner via Climate Control Projects. The podcast ran to 16 episodes across two seasons. Named after Roxane Gay's Bad Feminist.

Listen on Apple Podcasts

#PassTheMic — Attenborough Instagram campaign

Campaign

November 2020. Led a global campaign urging David Attenborough to hand his 6.1M-follower Instagram (which had accumulated 45 million video plays in record time) to frontline climate activists. Covered by BBC My World (executive produced by Angelina Jolie), Euronews, and outlets across 15+ countries.

▶
2021

Climate Live — organiser

Campaign

Youth-led climate concert movement officially launched 24 April 2021, simultaneously in 60+ countries. Co-founder Frances Fox is a close collaborator; Tori involved as organiser from launch.

Pass the Mic initiative — formally launched

Campaign

Co-launched with Dominique Palmer, Frances Fox, George Steedman Jones and Elijah McKenzie-Jackson. Targeted brands, celebrities and NGOs to spotlight MAPA (Most Affected People and Areas) activists, including the 'Reclaiming Our Time' campaign during Black History Month.

Billie Eilish's Overheated documentary

Media

Featured speaking on mental health and the climate crisis. Directed by Yassa Khan. Cast includes Vivienne Westwood, Vanessa Nakate, Girl in Red, Yungblud, Arlo Parks, Sofía Jannok and Finneas O'Connell — distributed to Billie Eilish's then 100M+ global audience.

▶

#StopCambo protest — TED Countdown, Edinburgh

Activism

Led protest against Shell CEO Ben van Beurden at TED Countdown in Edinburgh, as part of the campaign against North Sea oil and gas expansion ahead of COP26 in Glasgow. A precursor to her later Stop Rosebank work.

COP26, Glasgow — NYT Climate Hub panel

Landmark

November 2021. Attended as activist. Invited by Emma Watson to speak at the New York Times Climate Hub panel alongside Greta Thunberg, Malala Yousafzai, Amanda Gorman, Vanessa Nakate, Dominique Palmer and Mya-Rose Craig — filmed and distributed globally by the New York Times. The last COP she attended; she has since declined COP27, 28, 29 and 30, citing the UN's loss of credibility.

▶

Cosmopolitan Positivity Index 2021

Recognition

Named in Cosmopolitan UK's annual Positivity Index (May 2021 issue), in partnership with Instagram.

2022

EarthPercent — Lead Climate Advisor / Climate Justice Lead

Career

Brian Eno's music and climate organisation, directing a percentage of music streaming revenue to frontline climate projects. An ongoing advisory role bridging climate justice and the music industry — the partnership that later led to Together for Palestine.

Fossil Fuel Non-Proliferation Treaty — Treaty Champion

Campaign

Joined from September 2022. The initiative calls for a global treaty to manage the phase-out of fossil fuels in line with 1.5°C. Role later upgraded to Senior Advisor in 2024, overseeing comms and champions strategy globally — including trips to the G20 in Brazil.

Palestine advocacy — early and consistent

Activism

Bad Activist Collective posts about Angela Davis, Sheikh Jarrah and Israeli apartheid from 2022 onwards drew significant backlash within the climate space — predating October 7th by years.

One of our pillars was a free Palestine.

Marie Claire Future Shapers — 'Eco Warrior' 2022

Recognition

Named in Marie Claire UK's Future Shapers 2022 list, celebrated as the issue's Eco Warrior, recognising her intersectional approach to climate activism.

Marie Claire UK

British Vogue digital cover with Billie Eilish

Media

Aug 2023

It's Not Just You — published (UK, Simon & Schuster)

Book

13 August 2023. Debut book reframes eco-anxiety as a structural mental health crisis rather than an individual failing. Features interviews with Greta Thunberg, Vanessa Nakate and Mikaela Loach. Received immediate critical acclaim on publication.

Waterstones Book of the Year 2023 · Town & Country Future Leaders · ELLE Green List

Recognition

It's Not Just You named a Waterstones Book of the Year (Popular Science). Also named in Town & Country House Future Leaders and the inaugural ELLE UK Green List in the same year — recognition across mainstream media, literary and fashion spheres simultaneously.

2024

FFNPT — Senior Advisor (role upgraded)

Career

Full Senior Advisor role, overseeing comms and champions strategy globally. Represented the Treaty Initiative at the G20 in Brazil — and later on stage with Massive Attack at Co-op Live.

ELLE Big Green List 2024

Wainwright Prize 2024 — shortlisted

Recognition

Shortlisted in the Writing on Conservation category. Shortlist announced 15 August 2024; winners' ceremony at Camley Street Natural Park, London, 11 September 2024. Shortlisted only — Fire Weather by John Vaillant won — but a significant literary recognition for a debut author.

Climate Fiction Prize — inaugural judge

Media

Joined the judging panel for the inaugural Climate Fiction Prize (launched June 2024, Hay Festival), run by Climate Spring. Fellow judges: Madeleine Bunting (chair), David Lindo, Nicola Chester and Andy Fryers. Winner announced May 2025: Abi Daré's And So I Roar.

Jan 2025

The Tea with Dr Myriam François — Season 2 premiere

Media

A clip from the episode — covering California wildfires, racial capitalism, greenwashing and Trump's Paris Agreement withdrawal — generated 12 million Instagram views. The largest single-clip social media reach of her career to date, demonstrating her ability to generate mass impact through others' platforms.

12M views

Stop Rosebank — court victory

Legal victory

30 January 2025. The Court of Session in Edinburgh ruled that the UK government's approval of the Rosebank and Jackdaw oil fields was unlawful. Tori worked as Partnerships Consultant throughout the campaign against Ithaca Energy and Equinor. Backed by 90,000+ members of the public.

Stop Rosebank case
Jun 2025

World Environment Day — Massive Attack & Andy Burnham sign FFNPT

Landmark

5 June 2025. The declaration was signed on the solarised rooftop of the Greater Manchester Combined Authority HQ. Tori was then invited on stage at Co-op Live — the UK's largest entertainment arena — to address 15,000 fans as part of Massive Attack's sold-out show. Her words from the stage:

The fossil fuel industry is a death machine, and we're all caught in its gears. The time for waiting is over.

Eco Age — advisory board member

Career

Joined the advisory board of Eco Age (the sustainability consultancy behind the Green Carpet Fashion Awards) on its relaunch under new ownership, June 2025. Completing a circle that began at the GCFA Young Leaders ceremony in 2023.

Sep 2025

Together for Palestine — OVO Wembley Arena, London

Landmark

17 September 2025. Involved in organising Brian Eno's landmark benefit concert at OVO Wembley Arena — art directed by Palestinian painter Malak Mattar. Performers included PinkPantheress, James Blake, Jamie xx, Damon Albarn, Bastille and Sampha. Raised £1.5 million for Palestinian-led organisations, with 200,000+ livestream viewers alongside the sold-out 12,500-capacity arena.
