Step 1 — The pool

Every subscription goes into one global pot

"This kind of transparency is awesome. Just to share info from our side — none of the streaming services actually pay out per stream. We all pay out based on something called 'streamshare.' That means we take about 70% of all the money we make and put it in a royalty pool, and if you get 1% of the streams, you get 1% of the royalty pool... we try to keep your fans streaming!"

— Spotify for Artists, replying to Los Campesinos! streaming transparency post

Step 2 — The journey

Your money doesn't go to who you chose

Your listening choices are irrelevant to where your money goes.

Global stream share decides everything.

Step 3 — The formula

The pool is divided by who gets the most streams globally

Spotify's own formula

1% of streams
= 1% of pool

Every artist. Every month. Globally.

Taylor Swift's fans stream her music around the world 24 hours a day.

That is money moving away from every other artist on the platform. Including whoever you actually chose to listen to.

It doesn't matter that you never played a Taylor Swift song this month. She still got a share of your subscription.
